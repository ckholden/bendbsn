🧠 PHQ-9 Depression Screening ▶

"Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following?"
0 = Not at all 1 = Several days 2 = More than half the days 3 = Nearly every day
1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself, or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down
7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading or watching television
8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed, or the opposite — being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual
9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way
Severity: 0-4 None | 5-9 Mild | 10-14 Moderate | 15-19 Mod. Severe | 20-27 Severe
PHQ-9 Total: 0

🚨 C-SSRS Suicide Screening ▶

"Ask the patient each question directly."
1. Have you wished you were dead or wished you could go to sleep and not wake up?
2. Have you actually had any thoughts of killing yourself?
3. Have you been thinking about how you might do this?
4. Have you had these thoughts and had some intention of acting on them?
5. Have you started to work out or worked out the details of how to kill yourself? Do you intend to carry out this plan?
6. Have you ever done anything, started to do anything, or prepared to do anything to end your life?
Triage: All No = Negative screen | Q1-2 Yes = Low risk, refer | Q3-5 Yes = Moderate-High risk, immediate safety plan | Q6 Yes = Behavioral risk, immediate intervention

🍺 CAGE Alcohol Screening ▶

"Ask the patient each question."
C - Have you ever felt you should Cut down on your drinking?
A - Have people Annoyed you by criticizing your drinking?
G - Have you ever felt bad or Guilty about your drinking?
E - Have you ever had a drink first thing in the morning to steady your nerves or get rid of a hangover (Eye-opener)?
Scoring: 0-1 = Negative screen | 2+ = Clinically significant, further evaluation recommended

🛡️ Braden Scale (Pressure Injury Risk) ▶

Rate each subscale (lower score = higher risk)
Sensory Perception — Ability to respond to pressure-related discomfort
Moisture — Degree to which skin is exposed to moisture
Activity — Degree of physical activity
Mobility — Ability to change and control body position
Nutrition — Usual food intake pattern
Friction & Shear — Assistance required for movement
Risk: 19-23 No risk | 15-18 Mild risk | 13-14 Moderate risk | 10-12 High risk | 9 or below Very high risk

🔍 CAM Delirium Screening ▶

Assess patient for each feature. Delirium = Feature 1 AND 2, PLUS either 3 OR 4.
Feature 1: Acute Onset & Fluctuating Course
Is there evidence of an acute change in mental status from baseline? Does the abnormal behavior fluctuate during the day?
Feature 2: Inattention
Does the patient have difficulty focusing attention? Are they easily distracted or have difficulty keeping track of what is said?
Feature 3: Disorganized Thinking
Is the patient's thinking disorganized or incoherent? Rambling or irrelevant conversation, unclear or illogical flow of ideas, unpredictable switching between subjects?
Feature 4: Altered Level of Consciousness
Overall, how would you rate this patient's level of consciousness? (Alert = normal, anything else = abnormal: Vigilant, Lethargic, Stupor, Coma)
Interpretation: Positive = Feature 1 + Feature 2 + (Feature 3 OR Feature 4) → Delirium likely present
